Glossary of Trading Terms

  • Stock: Ownership in a company.
  • Dividend: A company's profit distribution to shareholders.
  • Bear Market: A market in decline, characterized by falling prices.
  • Bull Market: A market characterized by rising prices.
  • Short Selling: Selling a stock you don't own, with the intention of buying it back at a lower price.
  • Margin Trading: Borrowing money to trade stocks.
  • Volatility: A measure of how much a stock's price fluctuates.
  • Liquidity: How easily a stock can be bought or sold without affecting its price.
  • Resistance Level: A price level at which a stock tends to stop rising.
  • Support Level: A price level at which a stock tends to stop falling.
  • Market Capitalization: The total value of a company's outstanding shares.
  • PE Ratio: Price-to-earnings ratio, a measure of a stock's valuation.
  • Volume: The number of shares traded in a stock.
  • Index: A benchmark that tracks the performance of a group of stocks.
  • ETF: Exchange-traded fund, a collection of stocks or bonds traded on an exchange.
  • Options: Contracts that give the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell a stock at a specific price.
  • Futures: Contracts to buy or sell an asset at a future date for a predetermined price.
  • Short Squeeze: A situation where short sellers are forced to buy back shares, driving the stock price higher.
  • Market Maker: A firm that provides liquidity by buying and selling securities.
  • Day Trader: A trader who buys and sells stocks within the same trading day.
  • Swing Trader: A trader who holds stocks for a few days to a few weeks.
  • Position Trader: A trader who holds stocks for weeks to months.
  • Scalper: A trader who makes many small trades to profit from small price movements.
  • Algorithmic Trading: Trading done by computer algorithms based on predefined rules.
  • Technical Analysis: Analysis of stock price charts and indicators to make trading decisions.
  • Fundamental Analysis: Analysis of a company's financial health and market conditions to determine stock value.
  • Market Sentiment: The overall feeling or attitude of investors towards a market or stock.
  • Volatility Index (VIX): A measure of market volatility and investor sentiment.
  • Arbitrage: Profiting from price differences of the same asset in different markets.
  • Market Timing: The strategy of buying and selling stocks based on predictions of market movements.
  • Market Order: An order to buy or sell a stock at the current market price.
  • Limit Order: An order to buy or sell a stock at a specific price or better.
  • Stop Order: An order to buy or sell a stock once it reaches a certain price.
  • Trailing Stop Order: A stop order that adjusts with the stock price to lock in profits or limit losses.
